# User Experience Principles

## 1. Explain the frame before asking for trust

Tell users what information is being requested, why it matters, and whether it describes long-term tendencies, recent experience, or recommendation context.

## 2. Keep nature and current balance separate

Always pair each concept with time language:

- **Your nature:** tendencies across adult life when generally well
- **Your current balance:** self-reported experience during a recent period

Do not distinguish the concepts through color alone.

## 3. Make progress finite, not coercive

Show question number, approximate total, section, and save status. Permit uncertainty, skipping, and exit. Avoid streaks, countdowns, loss framing, and language that suggests an unfinished person.

## 4. Prefer one clear decision at a time

Use one question per screen, large single-select answers, concise help, and a stable Continue action. Profile setup should use two or three short steps rather than one dense form.

## 5. Save first, reassure second

Persist each answer immediately. Show a quiet “Saved” state without blocking or celebratory interruption. Recovery from network failure must preserve the local selection and explain what remains unsaved.

## 6. Make uncertainty visible without medicalizing it

Use stages such as Preliminary, Developing, and Well established. Describe completeness and freshness, not diagnostic confidence. Avoid unjustifiably precise percentages.

## 7. Keep content status explicit and AI separate

The limited MVP selects clearly labeled provisional guidance through deterministic rules and keeps AI unavailable. Future reviewed content must replace provisional items before they are described as expert-approved.

## 8. Keep daily value actionable

Today should answer three questions quickly:

1. What is relevant today?
2. Why might it be relevant to me?
3. What is one practical thing I can try?

## 9. Design safety as part of the flow

Use wellness boundaries where decisions occur, not only in a footer. Sensitive questions need skip paths; results need educational framing; AI needs visible limits and failure states.

## 10. Accessibility is interaction quality

Use logical reading order, programmatic labels, keyboard support, visible focus, 44 px minimum targets, non-color cues, concise headings, and content that survives 200% zoom and 320 px reflow.
